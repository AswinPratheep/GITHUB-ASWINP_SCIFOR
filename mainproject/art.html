Featured Artworks

The Starry Night

By Vincent van Gogh, 1889. One of the most recognized pieces of art in the world, The Starry Night depicts a dreamy interpretation of the artist's asylum room's sweeping view.

Mona Lisa

By Leonardo da Vinci, 1503. The Mona Lisa is famous for its mysterious smile and the subject's enigmatic expression, making it one of the most iconic paintings in history.

The Scream

By Edvard Munch, 1893. An iconic piece symbolizing existential angst and anxiety, The Scream is one of the most haunting artworks in history.

Girl with a Pearl Earring

By Johannes Vermeer, 1665. This captivating portrait is often referred to as the "Mona Lisa of the North" and is known for its delicate use of light and color.

The Last Supper

By Leonardo da Vinci, 1498. This famous mural painting represents the dramatic moment when Jesus reveals that one of his disciples will betray him.

American Gothic

By Grant Wood, 1930. Known for its depiction of a stern farmer and his daughter, this painting reflects rural American life during the Great Depression.

The Night Watch

By Rembrandt van Rijn, 1642. This painting is famous for its dramatic use of light and shadow, depicting a militia company in motion.

The Birth of Venus

By Sandro Botticelli, 1486. This masterpiece depicts the goddess Venus emerging from the sea on a shell, symbolizing beauty and love.
